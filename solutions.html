Solution Architect

A solution architect, sometimes called a system architect, is an information technology professional who designs programs and systems to meet the needs of an organisation and integrates with its current IT environment.

What does a solution architect do?



Solution architects work to improve an organisation's efficiency and effectiveness through computer-based initiatives.

Their duties include:

1. Examining current systems architecture to identify areas of improvement

2. Recommending system upgrades.

3. Developing new networking systems and processes.

4. Testing systems for proper functionality and running performance reports.

5. Troubleshooting technology issues and repairing problems.

6. Collaborating with developers, system analysts and clients to determine business needs and effective strategies.

7. Integrating new hardware, software and other technological devices.

8. Monitoring the implementation of new systems.

9. Defining timelines, budget and project scope for the IT team and clients.

10. Assessing a company's technological vulnerabilities.

11. Selecting particular frameworks, platforms and tech stacks for each project.

12. Designing how applications will look and function.

What skills do you need to be a solution architect?


Here are some skills solution architects use:

Technical knowledge


A solution architect has to have the technical knowledge and experience to consult on existing IT environments and make recommendations. They use their technical knowledge to assess a current system, identify weaknesses and create additions to systems when required. They need a working knowledge of the construction of hardware and software to provide workable solutions to their clients.

Communication skills


Solution architects work with clients, which means they are often working with people who may not have the same education in technology as them. They should be able to communicate effectively with their clients so they can understand their assignments and explain what changes need to be implemented. They use their communication skills to explain hard technical concepts to others and create a plan for improvement to which their client can agree.



Analytical skills


A solution architect uses their analytical skills to observe problems, find their origin and develop workable technological solutions. They identify how different tasks and operations affect each other and how they can alter different layers of business to create a better technological system for a company.



Project management skills


Solution architects usually work with a variety of clients whose systems require multiple solutions. This means they use project management skills to meet various deadlines and keep track of multiple parts of each project. They use organisation and time management to stick to their schedules, set goals and provide direction for how to meet them.
